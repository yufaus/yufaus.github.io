Completed Projects

Pill Dispenser

Explanation

A pill dispenser for family members' needs. Dispenses the same amount of supplements per operation. The priority of this dispenser was speed, given that the same number of the same types of tablets were to be dispensed every time.

Designed from scratch, it uses a 4-bar linkage connected to a servo to drive and agitate the dispenser, which fills up with tablets from a custom-designed funnel that went through many iterations to perfect. The brains of the device is an Arduino Nano connected to a shield for better pin access.

  • The machine waits for a container to be sensed by the ultrasonic sensor
  • It then moves the sliding tray to the drop position, agitates the pills out, and moves the tray back.
  • It then waits for the container to be removed, and then repeats the process.

Skills

This project has expanded my abilities in CAD design, assembly and integration with commercial parts in SOLIDWORKS, designing for the tolerances of 3D printing, using Ultimaker Cura to slice STL files for my Ender 3 V2 3D printer. It has also given me insight into the rapid prototyping approach, as I quickly went through many iterations of funnel design to arrive at my final one. I also had to adapt to changing requirements.

Current projects

PWM controlled Desktop vacuum cleaner

Idea

The aim of this project is to create a desktop vacuum cleaner for my workshop needs. Annoying trimmed wire ends and housings get in the way, and sweeping them into the bin is a chore.

Skills

Through this project, I hope to be able to develop my skills in analog circuit design, digital logic circuits, mechanical design, 3D print manufacturing, pulse-width modulation (PWM) and broaden my understanding of electronics. I hope to also gain insight into cyclone separators in vacuum cleaners, as well as fan blade design.
