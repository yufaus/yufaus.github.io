Current projects

Zero-software, PWM controlled, focused-illumination-area linkage lamp

Idea

The aim of this project is to create a desk lamp that uses a conventional lamp linkage, customized to my specifications of always pointing in the same direction regardless of its configured height. I plan to achieve this through a modified parallel linkage.

The lamp will light up my projects workbench through strong lamp-quality LEDs that are going to be driven by a PWM signal, generated by a 555 timer oscillator through a MOSFET, without the use of microcontrollers at all.

  • The user will be able to click buttons to cycle the light through 4 light level settings, stored by flip-flops. The light is also automatically adjusted to the current lighting conditions via a photodiode that varies the duty cycle of the PWM circuit.
  • The user can operate a two way switch to power a stepper motor, which drives gears to raise or lower the lamp linkage. The movement will be constrained by limit switches that prevent the lamp from breaking due to collisions.

Skills

Through this project, I hope to be able to develop my skills in analog circuit design, digital logic circuits, mechanical design, 3D print manufacturing, pulse-width modulation (PWM) and broaden my understanding of electronics.
